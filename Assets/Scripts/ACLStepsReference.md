# ACL Reconstruction — 4 Steps Reference

## Step 1: Patient Positioning & OR Setup
- **Phase**: Setup
- **Instruction**: "Start by flexing the knee at 30 degress."
- **Detail**: "Proper positioning is critical for arthroscopic access. A leg holder would allow the surgeon to manipulate the knee through a full range of motion during the procedure."

## Step 2: Femoral Tunnel Drilling
- **Phase**: FemoralTunnel
- **Instruction**: "Increase knee flexion to approximately 120°. Position the femoral drill guide at the identified footprint on the lateral femoral condyle. Drill the femoral tunnel."
- **Detail**: "The femoral tunnel is drilled through the anteromedial portal or trans-tibially, depending on technique. The guide targets the center of the femoral footprint. Tunnel depth is typically 25-30mm for suspensory fixation."

## Step 3: Tibial Tunnel Drilling
- **Phase**: TibialTunnel
- **Instruction**: "Position the tibial drill guide at approximately 55° angle, targeting the center of the tibial ACL. Drill the tibial tunnel."
- **Detail**: "The tibial guide pin should exit intra-articularly at the center of the native ACL tibial footprint. Typical guide angle is 50-55°. The tunnel diameter matches the graft size (usually 8-9mm for hamstring grafts)."

## Step 4: Graft Passage & Fixation
- **Phase**: GraftInsertion
- **Instruction**: "Pass the graft through the tibial tunnel and into the femoral tunnel. Apply tension and secure fixation on both sides with the knee at 30° of flexion."
- **Detail**: "The prepared hamstring graft is pulled through using sutures passed through both tunnels. Femoral fixation is applied first (suspensory button or interference screw), then the graft is tensioned and tibial fixation is applied with the knee at 30° of flexion."
